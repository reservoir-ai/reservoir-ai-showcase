# Vision

Reservoir engineering workflows often require significant manual effort to validate models, investigate operational changes, interpret results, and communicate uncertainty.

Modern engineering teams increasingly require tools that combine traditional reservoir engineering methods with automation, analytics, and artificial intelligence.

Reservoir AI aims to augment engineers by providing:

- Faster model validation
- Automated diagnostics
- Improved engineering visibility
- Uncertainty-aware decision support
- Natural language interaction with engineering data

The long-term objective is to create an engineering copilot capable of understanding reservoir models, operational history, engineering studies, and uncertainty information in order to accelerate technical decision making.

Reservoir AI is being developed as a practical engineering platform where physics-based understanding, domain expertise, and artificial intelligence work together to support better decisions.
